Instructions

Purpose: Assemble the evidence and decisions from Weeks 4.6-4.8 that plug straight into your Product Innovation Charter.

What is a PIC: A short strategic brief that sets the guardrails for an innovation project. It answers who we serve, where we will compete, the value we must deliver, and the constraints we must respect.

Note: When we say segment here we mean consumer segment unless we explicitly say product segment.

This prep work feeds directly into your Week 5 PIC development. Keep each line short and link to evidence from your Week 4 work.

The Five PIC Guardrails You Will Develop

  1. Who we serve: Consumer segment and key JTBD
  2. Where will we play: Product segment and white space target
  3. Value we must deliver: Non-negotiable benefits from pains and JTBD
  4. Feasibility guardrails: Design and technology constraints
  5. Risks and watch-outs: Key assumptions and mitigation steps
